What is Kai?
Kai brings an investment committee in silicon to every iPhone. On any stock or ETF, three specialist agents analyze, debate, and deliver a Buy/Hold/Reduce decision with receipts: sources, math, dissent, and risk-persona fit.
This is a faithful consumer translation of the AlphaAgents framework:
  • Specialized agent roles
  • Group-chat orchestration
  • Round-robin debate
  • Risk personas
  • Complete observability
Why Now?
Proven Performance
The AlphaAgents paper shows multi-agent debate improves decision quality and mitigates hallucinations. In risk-neutral tests, the multi-agent portfolio outperformed single-agent baselines.
Risk Management
Even in risk-averse mode, the multi-agent approach reduced drawdowns vs. single agents, demonstrating robust performance across risk profiles.
Explainable AI Demand
Consumers and advisors want explainable AIdecisions with a reasoning trail, not just a rating or black-box recommendation.
Kai responds to this opportunity by bringing institutional-grade decision processes to individual investors through a consumer-friendly interface.
The Multi-Agent Framework
We instrument the exact pattern from the AlphaAgents research: specialized roles with specific tools, round-robin debate until consensus, and comprehensive trust artifacts that document the entire process.
The Three Specialist Agents
Fundamental Agent
Analyzes 10-K/10-Q filings and financial reports using specialized RAG retrieval. Focuses on business fundamentals, financial health, and long-term viability.
Sentiment Agent
Processes news, earnings calls, and market sentiment through reflection summarization. Captures market momentum and short-term catalysts.
Valuation Agent
Performs quantitative analysis using deterministic math calculators. Evaluates metrics like P/E ratios, returns, volatility, and relative valuation.
Each agent brings specialized expertise and tools to the decision process, mirroring how human investment committees function.
Debate is the key to quality.
Every agent speaks at least twice. Consensus is required. No single agent may decide alone.

The orchestrated debate:

  • Creates a comprehensive audit trail
  • Improves decision quality

The Decision Card
The culmination of the debate process is a comprehensive Decision Card that includes:
  • Headline recommendation (Buy/Hold/Reduce + confidence)
  • Specialist insights from each agent
  • Debate digest highlighting key discussion points
  • Sources and mathematical evidence
  • Reliability badge indicating trustworthiness
  • Risk-persona alignment indicators
Goals and Non-Goals
Goals

Reliability Badge
The Reliability Badge provides at-a-glance confidence in the quality of each analysis based on:
  • RAG Faithfulness (0-1): How accurately the agents represent retrieved information

  • Tool Adherence (%): Whether mathematical calculations follow deterministic formulas

This transparency helps users appropriately calibrate their trust in each recommendation.

System Architecture Overview
The architecture balances on-device and cloud components to optimize for performance, cost, and privacy while delivering institutional-grade analysis capabilities.
Client Components
iOS Native App
SwiftUI app with Share Extension, Widgets, and App Intents (Siri) integration. Utilizes BGTaskScheduler and Background App Refresh for Alpha Digest generation.
Progressive Web App
Installable PWA for demo and read-only mode, providing cross-platform accessibility without the full native experience.
App Clip
Lightweight instant experience for "analyze this" try-before-you-download functionality, reducing friction for new users.
Important iOS note: Apple does not grant "root-level" permissions to third-party apps. We'll use sanctioned capabilities (Share Extension, Background App Refresh, BGTaskScheduler, Push, Widgets, App Intents). Any deeper background automation must be user-initiated or push-triggered.
Service Components
Flow (Orchestrator)
Manages the agent graph and group chat, enforcing debate rules and ensuring each agent speaks at least twice before consensus.
Agents
Fundamental Vault (10-K/10-Q RAG + report-pull)

Risks & Mitigations
Data Coverage Variability
Risk: Inconsistent availability of quality data across different securities
Mitigation: Degrade gracefully; annotate confidence levels; avoid pure sentiment portfolios when coverage is thin
Prompt-Only Risk Drift
Risk: Risk personas implemented via prompts may drift or be inconsistent
Mitigation: Pair persona prompts with hard constraints; log and flag violations
Back-Test Bias
Risk: Historical performance may not predict future results
Mitigation: Present cards as signals, not guarantees; show half-life/confidence; follow paper's stance on optimization
Platform Limitations
Risk: iOS restrictions on background processing
Mitigation: Use Apple-approved background modes; obtain user consent for notifications
Acceptance Criteria (V1)
